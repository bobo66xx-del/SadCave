## 🤔 Decisions

Four new entries in `_Decisions.md`:

- **Title sits ABOVE the name on the nametag, not below.** Title-as-epigraph, not title-as-metadata. Matches the "sits in the name like someone actually cares" felt brief and Tone Rule 6 better than the stacked-label shape. Lower-tier titles (`new here`, `settling in`) read hospitable in lowercase-soft rather than rank-y.
- **Polished menu is a right-side drawer with world-visible-dimmed-no-blur behind.** The cave doesn't disappear when the menu opens; the player can watch their own nametag shift in real time as they choose. Right-side over bottom (XPBar conflict) and over left (reading flow). No blur over soft blur (preserves cave continuity).
- **Locked-row hint voice goes mixed/contextual, not uniform.** Mechanical for level/gamepass/presence/exploration; indirect-poetic for achievement (12 hand-written lines) and seasonal. Tyler delegated the call after the four uniform options each had real costs (mechanical loses tone; uniform-atmospheric loses information; uniform-poetic over-precious for level rows).
- **Roll the duplicate-`TitleConfig`/`TitleRemotes` Studio cleanup into the polished-pass brief instead of a separate hygiene brief.** The polish brief already touches the Title surface heavily; the cleanup costs a few minutes inside that brief vs a full brief-write + Codex sitting + review cycle. Verify-before-delete + flag-and-stop protocol baked into Step 8 mitigates the wrong-deletion risk.

## 📝 Notes

- **First "design-only-no-code" session of this run.** Re-orient + design pass + brief writing, all on the vault side. Worked because the design surface (polished menu + nametag visual) is genuinely a Tyler-led-taste decision, not a code-architecture decision; there was no code to write because the spec doesn't ship until Codex picks up the brief. The pattern is reusable: when the next decision is "what does this feel like" (polished menus, NPC dialogue voice, area discovery feedback), a vault-only session is the right shape — leave the build cycle for when the design is locked.
- **Five rounds of AskUserQuestion to land the polished design.** Each round narrowed without backtracking. Format that worked: surface 3-4 distinct directions per shape question, with the descriptions covering what's *gained* and what's *lost* by each option. Tyler picked decisively on each (with one "no preference" delegated to Claude on hint voice).
- **The "roll cleanup into the brief" pattern is worth keeping in mind.** Studio-state hygiene items that surface during review (duplicate instances, stale modules, abandoned UI) are cheap to bundle into the next brief that's already touching the same area. Standalone hygiene briefs are heavy for what's typically two `Destroy()` calls. Build it into the verify-before-delete + flag-and-stop protocol so the deletion isn't blind.
- **PR #20 was merged silently between sessions** (~25 minutes after session 1 wrap). The session 1 recap predicted this and asked next-session integration to backfill — that prediction landed correctly. The "leave a clear trail for next session integration" pattern means the backfill was one focused 5-minute job rather than a "wait, what shipped?" puzzle. Worth keeping the explicit "next-session integration backfills with `HH:MM UTC` + PR #" sentence in any session recap that wraps before a Codex PR lands.
- **The duplicate-`TitleConfig` situation has lived in `_Open_Questions.md` for one full session now.** Bundling it into the polish pass means it gets resolved naturally; if the polish pass is delayed for any reason, the duplicate situation extends. Worth noting if the brief sits queued for more than a session or two — at that point a standalone cleanup brief becomes the right call.
